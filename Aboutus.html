About DUPC



Durham University Polo Club was formed in 1996, with the aim of offering an accessible and more affordable way for students to play polo. The ‘sport of kings’ has very much become the ‘sport of students’ – with Nationals being one of the largest student sporting events in the UK.



We are a highly successful team that compete and win in a range of different divisions - some perfectly designed for complete beginners and new riders, so no prior experience necessary. One of our new players last year, who had never ridden before, went on to place third at Summer Nationals, and another competed in Nigeria!





Now we are also one of the most accessible polo teams, with our free additional trainings in Durham. Under our Skills and Strength sessions we hold weekly: Polo Pilates, Strength & Conditioning with an MC coach, Bike Polo, Wooden Horse, and Tactics. These will help you to perfect your swing, balance, tactical thinking, team work, communication, core strength, coordination and muscle strength. Making sure you make the absolute most of your on-horse training.





In addition to providing high level training and opportunities to compete nationally and internationally, we have an extremely lively and inclusive social community that provides socials and events year round; from our annual Polo Ball in Epiphany term, to our inaugural Durham Champagne Polo Tournament in June.





Whatever you desire from DUPC, we have a variety of memberships to fit. So whether you want to play competitively as a DU athlete, causally play in your spare time, or just come for the incredible socials - we have it all! We pride ourselves on being a true polo family. We are not just a sports team; we are a community. A community that you can be a part of in so many different ways.
